Google Ads vs. Meta: Where to Put Your Budget?

By Reuben Hester • Nov 30, 2025

It is the most common question we get during discovery calls: "Should we start with Google or Facebook?" The answer isn't about which platform is "better"—it's about where your customer is in their lifecycle.

1. Intent (Google) vs. Discovery (Meta)

Google Ads is demand capture. People are searching for "luxury leather handbag" or "video production agency dublin." They are arguably closer to the bottom of the funnel. Conversion rates are higher, but scale is limited by search volume.

Meta Ads is demand generation. People aren't looking for you; they are scrolling through cat videos. You have to interrupt them. This requires stronger creative, but the scale is effectively infinite.

2. The High-Ticket Nuance

For high-ticket services (€5k+) or luxury goods, Google hits a ceiling fast. There aren't enough people searching for "€5,000 architectural lamp." You have to create the desire for it. That is where Meta wins. We find that for luxury brands, Meta builds the brand equity and "desire" that eventually leads to a branded Google search later.

3. The Ideal Mix

We typically recommend a 70/30 split for scaling brands:

  • 70% Meta (Top of Funnel): To reach new cold audiences and tell brand stories through video.
  • 30% Google (Bottom of Funnel): To capture the Branded Search traffic created by Meta, and high-intent generic keywords.
