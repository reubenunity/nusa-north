ROAS vs. POAS: The Vanity Metric Trap

By Reuben Hester • Dec 12, 2025

For years, Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) has been the holy grail of digital marketing relationships. But in 2025, with rising CPMs and tighter margins, ROAS has become a "vanity metric." Leading high-ticket brands are aggressively shifting to Profit on Ad Spend (POAS) to ensure growing revenue doesn't mean shrinking margins.

1. The ROAS Illusion in 2025

ROAS only looks at revenue generated vs. ad spend. It ignores COGS (Cost of Goods Sold), shipping, taxes, and operational overhead. If you have low margins, a "profitable" 4.0 ROAS on the dashboard might be net negative in the bank. We often see brands scaling campaigns that bleed cash simply because the top-line number looked good.

2. Enter POAS (The Truth Metric)

POAS tracks gross profit attributed to ads, divided by ad spend. This is the only way to scale safely in a high-cost environment.

The Formula:
(Revenue - COGS - Shipping - Taxes) / Ad Spend = POAS

A POAS > 1 means you are actually profitable. This nuance is critical for high-ticket e-commerce where variable costs (shipping insurance, premium packaging) are significant.

3. Profit-Based Bidding

The biggest shift in 2024/25 is that ad platforms like Google and Meta now support profit-based bidding. By feeding "Gross Profit" data back into the algorithm (via tools like server-side tracking), we train the AI to find profitable customers, not just any customers. This often changes the winning creative strategy entirely—prioritizing high-margin bundles over low-margin entry products.
