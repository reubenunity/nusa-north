How to Produce UGC That Doesn't Look Cheap

By Reuben Hester • Nov 12, 2025

We all know User Generated Content (UGC) works. It feels authentic, native, and trustworthy. But for a luxury brand, a shaky iPhone video in a messy bedroom can actualy detroy brand equity. The challenge is balancing "authentic" with "aspirational."

1. The "Polished UGC" Framework

We recruit creators not just for their audience, but for their aesthetic. We provide detailed briefs that specify:

  • Lighting: Natural window light or controlled studio light only. No yellow bedroom lamps.
  • Background: Minimalist, clean environments. Marble countertops, textured walls, or outufoors. No messy shelves.
  • Stabilization: Handheld is fine, but shaky is not. We encourage tripods for talking heads.

2. Scripting for Aspiration

Standard UGC says "OMG I love this thing, links in bio!" Luxury UGC says "I've been looking for a piece that compliments my routine..." The language must be elevated. We script the key talking points to ensure the value propositions (quality, material, longevity) are hit without sounding salesy.

3. Editing Matters

We often take the raw footage from creators and edit it in-house. We add our licensed fonts, precise cuts to music, and color grade the footage to remove unflattering tones. This hybrid approach gives you the trust of a face, with the polish of an agency.
